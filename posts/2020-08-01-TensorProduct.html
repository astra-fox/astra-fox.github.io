Tensor product and Universal property (draft)

Posted on August 1, 2020 by Alicia Henao
Tags: Linear Algebra, English

Definition 1: Let be \(\mathbb{V}\) a \(\mathbb{K}\)-vector space. And let be \(\phi: \mathbb{V} \times \mathbb{V} \rightarrow \mathbb{K}\) a function. We say \(\phi\) is a bilinear form, or bilinear function, if \(\phi\) is linear for each argument. This mean for all \(v, w, u \in \mathbb{V}\) and for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{K}\):
1. \(\phi(\alpha v + \beta w, u) = \alpha \phi(v, u) + \beta \phi(w, u)\)
2. \(\phi(v, \alpha w + \beta u) = \alpha \phi(v, w) + \beta \phi(v, u)\)


Definition 2: Let be \(\mathbb{V}\) a \(\mathbb{K}\)-vector space. And let be \(\phi: \mathbb{V} \times \mathbb{V} \rightarrow \mathbb{K}\) a bilinear form. We say \(\phi\) is symmetric bilinear form if for all \(v, w \in \mathbb{V}\) we have, \(\phi(v, w) = \phi(w, v)\).
